I am a PhD student supervised by Bart de Boer working on the ABACUS project. The main focus of my PhD has been on how signal spaces (or linguistic modalities) affect the dynamics of the emergence of structure in linguistic signals.

One of the main focuses in language evolution research in recent years has been how cognitive biases shape language via learning, cultural transmission and interaction. However, the main focus of the evolution of speech literature focuses on the evolution of the vocal tract. My work aims to create a bridge between these two disciplines, looking at how production, perception, transmission and interaction are affected by the physical aspects of a signalling modality. The spoken and signed modalities differ in their dimensionality, size, transparency and mappability. How these differences interface with linguistic development and evolution is important, not only in the context of researching real-world language evolution, but also in understanding the consequences of using signal-space proxies in artificial language learning experiments.
